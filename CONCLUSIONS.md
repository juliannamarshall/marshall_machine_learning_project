# Conclusions

## Key Findings
The analysis revealed that Airbnb listings in Los Angeles are driven more by internal property characteristics than by external crime rates.

---

## Main Insight
While crime varies across neighborhoods, it does not strongly determine Airbnb distribution or pricing. Instead, Airbnb listings are shaped by market demand, property features, and location desirability.

---

## Why This Matters
This challenges the assumption that safety is a primary driver of short-term rental behavior. Even in areas with higher crime rates, Airbnb activity can remain strong if demand is high.

---

## Additional Insights
- Airbnb listings naturally cluster into distinct groups
- A small number of features explain most of the data structure
- Machine learning models struggle when relationships are weak or indirect

---

## What I Learned
- Real-world data rarely produces strong predictive models
- Feature engineering and data preparation are critical
- Unsupervised methods (like clustering) can reveal more insight than supervised models

---
